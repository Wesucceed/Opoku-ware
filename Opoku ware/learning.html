Curriculum

Opoku Ware School has been expert in educating boys for over 10 years, which means we can draw on a wealth of experience in how boys learn best.

The curriculum of Opoku Ware School follows a structure which consolidates study over time and provides ample opportunity for cross-curricular learning. Students have access to a wide selection of science and math topics and are supported through their choice of GCSE and A level subjects aligning with their strengths and passions.

We believe that thinking should be “out of the box”, not “tick the box”. Our teachers encourage their students to become inspired learners with inquisitive, original mindsets. As experts in their fields, they share their infectious passion for their subjects to ensure every student is stretched and challenged, but also able to play to his strengths.

Year 1 Curriculum

In Years 1 and 2, Science lessons cover the three main branches of Science (Biology, Chemistry and Physics). You will have one teacher for all Sciences, to allow you a broader insight of the history and philosophy of Science. We aim to include as many experiments and competitions as possible to make you feel more comfortable using scientific equipment, as well as to make you familiar with the scientific method.

Biology

In Biology, you will study the structure and functions of cells, the building blocks of life. You will also learn about how organisms reproduce, with an introduction to genetics.

Chemistry

In Chemistry, you will use the particle theory of matter to explain how materials can change. You will also learn about the chemical elements and how they form compounds, and you will study the properties of acids and alkalis.

Physics

In Physics, you will learn how forces can affect the motion of objects. You will also investigate how light travels from one place to another and why we see colour. You will find out more about the Earth’s place in the Solar System and the Universe beyond.

Math

  1. Pythagoras’ Theorem
  2. Trigonometry
  3. Scale drawing and bearings
  4. Circles
  5. Area and Volume

Year 2 Curriculum

In Year 2, the three Sciences (Biology, Chemistry and Physics) are taught together by one teacher. Pupils build on their knowledge from Year 7, and establish a firm foundation for their international GCSE studies

Biology

In Biology you will study the processes that maintain ecosystems and the ways in which organisms are adapted to their habitat. You will also learn about the inheritance of characteristics with an introduction to genetics.

Chemistry

In Chemistry you will investigate mixtures and different techniques to separate them. You will learn more about the Groups of the Periodic Table and the reactivity of different elements.

Physics

In Physics you will learn about the nature of electrical charge and investigate current and voltage in circuits. You will also learn about the effect of pressure, and how to describe the motion of objects. All of these topics are supported by a number of experiments. As the year progresses, you will develop your practicalskills and learn how to plan an investigation.

Math

  1. Polygons Correlation
  2. Algebra including Solving Equations and Rearranging Formulae Inequalities
  3. Transformations
  4. Straight Line Graphs

Year 3 Curriculum

Year 3 will be just about the revision and practise of all the topics taught in Year 1 and 2.

Science and Math Resources

Science lab

Opoku Ware School has an ultra-modern science lab that promote scientific education through experiments . The lab is well equipped with necessary lab resources that a high school student needs to engage in meaningful scientific experiments that can help them better understand the concepts the students are taught in classroom. All students are invited to use the science laboratory.

Science and Math library

Science and Math library: Opoku Ware School has an ultra-modern science and math library that promote scientific education through science and math books made available at the library. The library is well equipped with necessary science and math library resources such as books, computers, and other scientific tools that a high school student needs to engage in meaningful research that can help them better understand the concepts the students are taught in classroom. The library is open to all students.

Information and Technology lab

Opoku Ware School has an ultra-modern IT lab that promote scientific education( specifically computer science). The lab is well equipped with computers, printers, wifi, and projectors that a high school student needs to practise his or her IT and other scientific skills that can help them better understand the concepts the students are taught in classroom. All students are invited to use the IT laboratory.
